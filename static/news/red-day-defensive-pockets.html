Red day, but defensive pockets stayed resilient

Why it matters: weak markets still reveal where money is hiding.

A red closing day does not mean everything is weak. The useful question is simple: what fell sharply, and what refused to fall? That difference shows sector rotation and risk appetite.

What fellHigh-beta and crowded sectors can see faster selling when the index weakens. IT, metals, or momentum names often show pressure first.
What heldFMCG, pharma, utilities, and other defensive pockets may stay stable when traders move toward safety.

Simple takeaway:

Do not chase panic. Build a watchlist, observe which sectors hold support, and wait for confirmation. Educational only, not investment advice.
